Culture

Baleada

a traditional Honduran dish consisting of a thick flour tortilla folded and filled with refried beans, crema (Honduran sour cream), and crumbled queso duro (hard cheese), often topped with other ingredients like eggs, avocado, or meat.

Pupusas

a thick, griddled cornmeal (masa) or rice flour flatbread from El Salvador and Honduras, typically stuffed with savory fillings like cheese, beans, chicharrón (pork), or squash and served with tangy curtido (pickled cabbage slaw) and salsa

Tamales

A tamale, in Spanish tamal, is a traditional Mesoamerican dish made of masa, a dough made from nixtamalized corn, which is steamed in a corn husk or banana leaves. The wrapping can either be discarded prior to eating or used as a plate.

Honduran quesadillas

a unique sweet, moist, cheesy pound cake dessert.

honduran rosquillas

traditional, ring-shaped baked corn biscuits (cookies) or corn donuts, often made with cheese, corn masa, and sometimes lard or butter

Tajadas de plátano

fried plantain slices

Arroz con leche

a creamy dessert made from rice cooked with milk, sugar, and spices like cinnamon and vanilla, often served warm or chilled with toppings like raisins or nuts

Horchata

a traditional, non-alcoholic, and often plant-based beverage with various regional variations

Pescado frito

fried fish

Pollo chuco

a popular, hearty Honduran street food featuring crispy, zesty fried chicken served over a bed of tajadas (fried green plantain slices), topped with a tangy cabbage slaw, pickled red onions, and a creamy, spicy sauce (often mayo-ketchup-based)
